PLANE TRANSPORT

No matter where you live, flying to Canada is easy, as there are daily direct flights to many cities and connections to almost all parts of the country.

· Arrive Early: Avoid stress by arriving at the airport with enough time.

· Hydrate and Dress Comfortably: Drink water during the flight and wear comfortable clothes.

· Know Baggage Rules: Familiarize yourself with weight and liquid restrictions.

· In-Flight Exercises: Perform simple exercises to prevent stiffness on long flights.

BOAT TRANSPORT

With the world's largest coastline and concentration of freshwater lakes (not to mention the many routes on the rivers), boating is part of Canadian life.

· Safety First: Know safety measures and the location of life jackets.

· Weather Preparedness: Wear appropriate clothing for potential weather changes on the water.

· Prevent Seasickness: If prone to seasickness, consider preventive measures like wristbands or medications.

· Make the Most of Stops: Explore interesting destinations during the boat stops.

ROAD TRANSPORT

If you are from the Americas, Canada's open highways through stunning nature offer some of the best road trips in the world. Traveling by motorcycle, car, truck, or RV is a fantastic way to explore the country.

· Itinerary Planning: Plan strategic stops for rest and meals.

· Vehicle Maintenance: Check tires, brakes, and fluids before heading out

· Comfortable and Safe Driving: Adjust your seat, wear comfortable clothing, and safety rules.

· Entertainment and Breaks: Prepare music and make regular stops for rest.

WHAT DO YOU NEED TO TRAVEL

If you want to travel to Canada you need to know a few things before arriving in the country.

Visa & Taxes

As of December 1, 2016, you will no longer need a visa to visit Canada. Instead, you'll need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA).

As in all countries, we have rules about what you can enter and leave Canada. However, there is nothing to worry about, they are not very complicated.

Time Zones

Canada spans six time zones, from the Atlantic in Newfoundland and Labrador to the Pacific in British Columbia, creating up to a six-hour difference in local time.

This extensive geographical range influences the organization of the country and leads to unique day and night durations across Canada.

Languaje

French is an official language and is spoken in all provinces, although you can speak English perfectly in all of them. The largest concentration of native French speakers is in Quebec.

accomodation tips

  • Be clear about the type of accommodation you are looking for.

    In Canada, accommodation options range from hotels to unique choices like Paradores, with budget-friendly alternatives in pensions, hostels, and campsites outside tourist areas.

  • Check the location and available transportation options.

    Choose accommodation based on the address; city center is ideal for walking in tourist areas, while affordable options outside historical centers are well-connected to the center.

  • Read the comments from other travelers.

    Consider review dates and profiles, establishments may respond with useful information, and reviews can be filtered for a tailored perspective.

  • If you are traveling with children...

    In Canada, accommodations often offer childcare services, inquire about them when booking; additional services may include extra beds or cribs for children.

  • If you're accompanied by your pet

    Before booking, confirm if the accommodation allows pets, with restrictions often favoring smaller animals, and ensure you have all necessary pet documentation.
